A BRIEF ABOUT ME


I was born and grown up in a village (in Dhakadakshin, Golapganj) under Sylhet district of Bangladesh. There were several institutions from which I have got my elementary and high school education.

I have completed MSc (Thesis) and BSc (Honours) degree in Forestry from the Department of Forestry and Environmental Science, School of Agriculture and Mineral Sciences at Shahjalal University of Science and Technology, Sylhet, Bangladesh.

Currently, I am pursuing a Master of Science in Geo-Information Science and Earth Observation from the ITC Faculty of Geo-Information Science and Earth Observation, University of Twente, The Netherlands. My research interest is on the Remote Sensing (RS) and Geographic Information System (GIS) applications in forestry, land-use, and climate change.

I have had some spectacular extracurricular experiences, as a member of four different campus organizations. I administered a student organization, Green Explore Society, as the President on the 2014-15 term (3rd Executive Committee). The organization works in the domain of nature and environment (conservation, public awareness, and research). Furthermore, I am a founding member of another student association, Golap-Bandhan Student Association. I also worked there as the Vice-President on the 2014-15 term.
